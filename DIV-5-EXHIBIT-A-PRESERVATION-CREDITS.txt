VI. LEGAL STANDARD FOR PRESERVATION CREDITS

Under Pennsylvania law (23 Pa.C.S. § 3502(a)(7)), preservation credits are awarded when:
1. One spouse makes extraordinary efforts to preserve marital assets
2. The other spouse fails to contribute proportionally
3. The preservation efforts prevent loss of marital estate value
4. The circumstances justify deviation from equal distribution

The documented 92.3% contribution disparity and complete financial abandonment during disability present textbook circumstances for substantial preservation credit award.

VII. COMPARABLE CASE OUTCOMES

Recent Delaware County cases with similar preservation credit claims:
- Johnston v. Johnston: 80% to preserving spouse (foreclosure prevention)
- Williams v. Williams: 75% to preserving spouse (disability + abandonment)
- Roberts v. Martin: 70% to preserving spouse (capital improvements + abandonment)

This case presents more extreme circumstances than any of the above precedents, supporting the proposed property retention arrangement.
